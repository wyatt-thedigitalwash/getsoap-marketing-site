# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Franchisor decision-makers at PE-backed franchise portfolios: marketing directors, operations VPs, franchise development leadership. Multi-brand, multi-location, sophisticated business buyers.

They arrive mid-funnel, already contacted by Wyatt. They are evaluating whether Soap is credible and worth a demo conversation. They are not browsing or discovering. Context is laptop at a desk, comparing options, looking for specifics.

## Product Purpose

Soap is a multi-tenant marketing platform built for franchise systems. The marketing site at getsoap.app exists to confirm credibility and convert warm enterprise leads into demo conversations. Success is a completed contact form from a qualified franchise operator.

## Brand Personality

Confident, direct, modern. Speaks to sophisticated buyers without hype. Specific over abstract. Concrete over clever. No urgency tactics, no exclamation points, no filler.

Three words: **authoritative, precise, understated**.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS templates**: Wix/Squarespace SaaS templates, cookie-cutter startup pages with gradient blobs, stock photography hero sections, "Transform your business" headlines, 3-column icon grids.
- **Overdesigned agency sites**: Heavy parallax, animation-for-animation's-sake, award-show spectacle that prioritizes craft theater over substance. If it takes 4 seconds to load and 10 to understand, it's wrong.
- **Franchise industry incumbents**: Cluttered dashboards-as-marketing, enterprise-gray color schemes, "Request a Demo" popups on every page, trust-badge walls with no real proof.

## Design Principles

1. **Show the product, not the promise.** Styled component mockups, data visualizations, and real interface patterns carry more weight than abstract claims. Every section should make the buyer think "I can see how this works."

2. **Earned confidence.** The site's credibility comes from specificity: real numbers, concrete capabilities, named features. Not from volume, not from social proof theater, not from design excess.

3. **Quiet authority.** Premium polish that doesn't announce itself. The buyer should feel they're dealing with a serious operator, not a startup trying to look bigger than it is. Ramp, Mercury, Linear are the reference anchors.

4. **Franchise-native language.** Speak the buyer's operational reality: brands, locations, franchisees, brand standards, local SEO, territory coverage. Not generic SaaS vocabulary.

5. **Respect the buyer's time.** Mid-funnel visitors scan for confirmation, not education. Dense information, clear hierarchy, fast path to contact. No scroll-jacking, no mandatory journeys.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA compliance. 4.5:1 minimum contrast for body text, 3:1 for large text. Full keyboard navigation. Screen reader support with semantic HTML and ARIA labels. Reduced motion alternatives for all animations. Color is never the sole indicator of state.
